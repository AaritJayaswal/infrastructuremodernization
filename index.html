Bill Overview

A comprehensive approach to modernizing America's infrastructure for the 21st century

Purpose

Establish a robust, federally mandated framework for climate-resilient design and enhanced cybersecurity across all critical infrastructure projects and existing systems.

Key Goals

  • Mandatory climate resilience standards
  • Enhanced cybersecurity for critical infrastructure
  • Workforce development programs
  • Innovation and pilot programs

Why Now?

Our infrastructure faces unprecedented threats from climate change and cyberattacks. This bill moves beyond patching problems to building for the future.

The Challenge

Climate Threats

Extreme weather events, sea-level rise, and changing precipitation patterns threaten our infrastructure systems.

Cybersecurity Risks

Sophisticated cyberattacks target critical infrastructure, endangering public safety and national security.

Economic Impact

Infrastructure failures lead to catastrophic economic losses and degrade quality of life.

Section 1: Climate Resilience Standards

Mandatory climate resilience standards for federal infrastructure investment

1.1 Establishment of Standards

The Secretary of Transportation, in consultation with EPA and NOAA, shall develop and implement mandatory climate resilience standards for all new and significantly upgraded infrastructure projects receiving federal funding within 18 months of enactment.

  • Account for localized climate impact projections
  • Include sea-level rise considerations
  • Address extreme precipitation patterns
  • Plan for sustained drought conditions
  • Mitigate heat stress impacts

1.2 Resilient Design Requirements

All federal agencies providing infrastructure funding shall ensure recipient entities incorporate resilient design features as a condition of receiving federal funds.

Elevation of Critical Assets

Raise infrastructure above projected flood levels

Nature-Based Solutions

Utilize permeable surfaces, wetlands restoration, and mangrove protection

Climate-Robust Materials

Employ materials resistant to projected climate stressors

1.3 Climate Risk Assessments

All federal infrastructure funding applications must include comprehensive climate risk assessments detailing vulnerabilities and proposed mitigation strategies.

Section 2: Enhanced Cybersecurity

Comprehensive cybersecurity protection for critical infrastructure operations

2.1 Cybersecurity Protection Mandate

CISA, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish and enforce mandatory minimum cybersecurity performance requirements for all critical infrastructure entities within 12 months of enactment.

Required Security Measures:

  • Multifactor authentication
  • End-to-end encryption
  • Robust network segmentation
  • Regular vulnerability assessments
  • Incident response protocols

Covered Sectors:

Energy Water Systems Transportation Healthcare Communications Financial Services

2.2 Critical Infrastructure Cybersecurity Grant Program

$500 Million Annually for 5 Years

Administered by CISA to provide funding for:

  • State, local, and tribal governments
  • Privately owned critical infrastructure entities
  • Implementation of cybersecurity requirements
  • Advanced cybersecurity technologies and training

2.3 Incident Reporting and Information Sharing

Mandatory and timely reporting of significant cyber incidents affecting critical infrastructure to CISA, with enhanced threat intelligence sharing platform.

Benefits:

  • Secure, anonymous platform for threat sharing
  • Legal protections for shared information
  • Improved collective defense capabilities
  • Faster incident response coordination

Section 3: Workforce Development & Innovation

Building the skilled workforce needed for resilient infrastructure

3.1 Resilient Infrastructure Workforce Grants

$200 Million Annually for 5 Years

Administered by the Secretary of Labor in coordination with the Department of Education

Target Recipients:

  • Community colleges
  • Vocational schools
  • Apprenticeship programs

Training Focus Areas:

  • Climate-resilient infrastructure design
  • Construction and maintenance techniques
  • Cybersecurity for operational technology
  • Advanced materials and technologies

Priority Criteria:

  • Partnerships with local labor unions
  • Programs targeting underserved communities
  • Industry collaboration and job placement

3.2 Innovation and Pilot Program

$100 Million Annually for 3 Years

Administered by the Department of Commerce in partnership with relevant federal agencies

Innovation Focus Areas:

Advanced Materials

Next-generation materials for infrastructure resilience

Smart Grid Solutions

Intelligent grid systems for enhanced resilience

AI for Predictive Maintenance

Artificial intelligence for infrastructure monitoring

Next-Gen Cybersecurity

Advanced cybersecurity defenses for operational technology

Eligible Applicants:

  • State and local governments
  • Universities and research institutions
  • Private companies and startups
  • Public-private partnerships

Section 4: Funding & Oversight

Sustainable funding mechanism and accountability measures

4.1 Funding Mechanism

Corporate Stock Buyback Surtax

0.5%

surtax on all corporate stock buybacks exceeding $1 million in a single fiscal year

Projected Annual Revenue: $2 Billion

Deposited into the dedicated "National Infrastructure Resilience Fund"

Fund Allocation:

  • Cybersecurity Grant Program: $500M annually
  • Workforce Development Grants: $200M annually
  • Innovation Pilot Program: $100M annually
  • Agency implementation and oversight: $1.2B annually

4.2 Oversight and Accountability

Government Accountability Office (GAO) Oversight

  • Biennial audits and reports to Congress
  • Assessment of infrastructure resilience progress
  • Evaluation of cybersecurity posture improvements
  • Review of workforce development outcomes
  • Recommendations for program improvement

Accountability Framework:

Performance Metrics

Clear, measurable outcomes for each program

Transparency

Public reporting on fund usage and results

Regular Reviews

Biennial assessments with congressional oversight

Continuous Improvement

Adaptive management based on performance data
